Maintenance Schedule

Before Each Use

  1. Check your unit over for fuel / oil leakage
  2. Always add fresh fuel to your equipment's fuel tank
  3. Check engine oil level using the dipstick
  4. Check and clean air inlet screen with cloth

Every 25 Hours

  1. Check air filter
  2. Inspect blade spindle for cracks, replace with Original Equipment Manufactured (O.E.M.) parts only

Every 50 hours

  1. Check tire pressure. Proper inflation will extend service life
  2. Tighten hardware at spindles, steering joints, wheels, & frame
  3. Clean dust & dirt from cylinder head fins
  4. Change oil & replace oil filter

Every 100 Hours

  1. Replace air filter
  2. Check secondary air filter*

Annually

  1. Replace spark plug with the recommended spark plug
  2. Lubricate grease fittings, wheels bearings, deck lift pivot, & caster assemblies
  3. Clean battery terminals to reduce corrosive build-up & apply a thin layer of pertrolium jelly to each battery terminal
  4. Inspect belts for wear & replace with O.E.M. parts as needed

Prior to Storage

  1. Run the unit dry of fuel or use a fuel stabilizer for storage. Follow the instructions regarding the fuel stabilizer
  2. Remove battery & place it on a trickle charger. Clean battery terminals

    3. * If applicable.

    For more detailed instructions refer to your operator's manual

Diagnose & Troubleshoot

Common Symptoms:

  • Engine fails to start
  • Engine idles rough
  • Engine overheats
  • Engine runs erratically
  • Engine skips (hesitates) at high speed
  • Excessive vibration
  • Mower does not mulch when using mulch kit
  • Uneven cut
